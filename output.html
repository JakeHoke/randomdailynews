Witnesses say he was doing it wrong, but with extraordinary confidence

·

"I just sort of moved my arms and legs around and I didn't sink," Poffington told reporters gathered in his driveway. "Nobody's ever thought of this. I checked. Well, I didn't actually check, but it feels original."

His wife, Denise, was unavailable for comment as she was inside the house doing what she called "pretending to be asleep."

Despite attempts by neighbors, historians, and a mildly confused lifeguard to explain that swimming has existed for approximately 10,000 years, Poffington remained resolute. The patent office confirmed they had received his application and were, in their words, "reviewing it with the gravity it deserves." An insider described this as "not at all."

As of press time, Poffington had also announced plans to invent sunscreen and "that thing where you close your eyes underwater."
